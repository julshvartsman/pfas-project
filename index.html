Component 01
The Map

Interactive 3D Explorer

A choropleth layer shades each geographic unit by demographic or vulnerability profile on a blue → orange scale. 3D bars extrude from each boundary to encode PFAS contamination — darker orange, taller bars signal communities carrying the greatest burden with the fewest resources.

Reading the map

Each unit shaded by chosen demographic variable. 3D bars encode PFAS contamination simultaneously — darker orange rising high marks the most burdened communities.

Data pipeline

EPA ECHO measurements spatially joined to ACS geometries. Filtered to confirmed detections in public water systems, aggregated to the selected geographic unit.

Redundant encoding

Color and bar height are two independent signals — so colorblind users can read contamination levels from height alone without relying on hue.

Component 02
The Record

Data & Analysis

A guided walkthrough pairing a timeline of PFAS spread with interactive exploratory visualizations — scatter plots, detection trends, and demographic breakdowns that surface the patterns underlying contamination data.

Regulatory Timeline
1940s
Industrial synthesis begins

3M develops PFOS; DuPont patents PTFE (Teflon). Widespread manufacturing adoption. Early internal toxicity data suppressed from public and regulators.

1998
Internal evidence shared with EPA

3M data reveals PFOS accumulates in human blood globally. No public disclosure follows. Voluntary phase-out negotiated years later — no enforcement.

2001–06
C8 Health Project, Parkersburg WV

DuPont's Teflon plant linked to elevated cancer rates in surrounding community. Predominantly low-income and rural. Class-action settlement with no admission of liability.

2016
First EPA health advisory — non-binding

Lifetime advisory set at 70 ppt combined PFOA+PFOS. Voluntary only. No enforcement mechanism. Communities with contaminated systems receive no legal recourse.

2022
PFAS Strategic Roadmap

EPA commits to binding Maximum Contaminant Levels. Decades of advocacy from contaminated communities precede this commitment.

2024
First federal MCL established

EPA sets MCL at 4 ppt for PFOA and PFOS — 80 years after these compounds entered widespread production. Compliance required by 2029.

EPA · ECHO
PFAS Analytic Tools

Live contamination measurements from public water systems nationwide. Basis for all PFAS exposure variables in this analysis.

U.S. Census Bureau
ACS 5-Year Estimates, 2020–2024

Demographic estimates at census tract level: race, income, poverty, age structure, rurality. Joined geographically to water system service areas.

CDC / ATSDR
Social Vulnerability Index, 2022

Composite burden indices across four themes: socioeconomic status, household characteristics, racial/ethnic minority status, and housing type.

Component 03
The Stories

Human & Community Impact

Quantitative patterns placed in human context. Integrating toxicological research, community testimony, and the social history of regulatory neglect to trace who decided which communities would carry this burden — and how that decision was made.
